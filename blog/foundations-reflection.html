Foundation Reflection

@EDA

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

I learnt that I don’t have to fixate on what I am already, I feel like I am seeing myself with more compassion and open for change and possibilities. I also feel much less competitive comparing to myself in the past, I have been enjoying my own learning and don’t really think about competition anymore later in foundation.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

values help forming a sense of who I am and how I choose to see things. Empathy enables me to be patient with myself and others, especially when things get frustrating, which is crucial to move forward.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

That I don’t have to stay in an obnoxious and cynical mindset, being curious and kind to myself and others is not “uncool”.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

I guess the entire process of reflecting on my past understanding of core learning is challenging. I didn’t realise how reluctant I felt at first. .

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Because anything, no matter how technical oriented, when put into context in the real world, concerns human as human? .

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

To be honest, I have felt that at times. But throughout the process I do see the importance and how core learning benefits me. So I don’t think it’s a waste of time.
